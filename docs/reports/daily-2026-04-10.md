## Decisions Made
- **Broadcast from controller, not model callbacks** — simpler for this scope, keeps serialization in one place (serialize_entry is already there)
- **actor_apikey instead of actor_id in broadcasts** — matches the frontend convention where user.id is the public apikey string; avoids exposing internal integer IDs
- **JWT in query param for WebSocket auth** — ActionCable doesn't support custom headers on the WebSocket handshake; query param is the standard Rails approach
- **Singleton consumer (not per-component)** — ActionCable manages its own WebSocket with auto-reconnect; multiple consumers would mean multiple connections per user
